SILVER PROOF COINS & BULLION
================================================================================

DESCRIPTION:
High-value precious metal coins produced by the Royal Australian Mint and 
Perth Mint

MAJOR SERIES & RELEASES:

GOLD COINS:
• 1981 - $200 Gold Coin (Charles & Diana Wedding)
• 1988 - $10 State Series Silver Proof
• 2018-2026 - $1 Mintmark Gold Coins (M, S, C, B, A)

SILVER KOOKABURRA SERIES (Perth Mint):
Years: 1990-2026 (ongoing)
Sizes: 1oz, 2oz, 5oz, 10oz, 1kg
Notes: Annual design changes, highly collectible
       First year (1990) particularly valuable

SILVER KOALA SERIES (Perth Mint):
Years: 1990-2026 (ongoing)
Sizes: 1oz (standard)
Notes: Annual design changes featuring koalas
       Popular investment and collectible series

LUNAR SERIES I & II (Perth Mint):
Years: 1996-2026 (ongoing)
Theme: Chinese zodiac animals
Designs: Dragon, Snake, Horse, Goat, Monkey, Rooster, Dog, Pig,
         Rat, Ox, Tiger, Rabbit (12-year cycle, now on Series III)
Sizes: 1/2oz, 1oz, 2oz, 5oz, 10oz, 1kg
Notes: Extremely popular with collectors worldwide
       Series I (1996-2007) more valuable than Series II

AUSTRALIAN KANGAROO/NUGGET SERIES:
• 2000-2026 - $1 Silver Kangaroo (1oz)
Notes: Annual design changes
       Originally "Nugget" series (gold), expanded to silver

COMMEMORATIVE SILVER PROOF SETS:
• 2001 - Federation Proof Silver Set (highly valuable)
• 2006 - Melbourne Commonwealth Games Silver
• 2012 - Year of the Dragon Silver (Lunar Series)
• 2015 - ANZAC Centenary Silver Set
• 2016 - 50th Anniversary Decimal Currency Silver Set
• 2020 - $1 Megafauna Set (Silver) - Prehistoric Australian animals
• 2022 - $2 Honey Bee Silver Proof

SPECIAL RELEASES:
• Annual Silver Proof Kookaburra (1990-present)
• Annual Silver Proof Koala (2007-present)
• Lunar Series (1996-present)
• Various commemorative silver dollars
• Olympic Games silver coins (2000, 2012, 2016, 2020, 2024)

VALUE FACTORS:
- Silver content and purity (usually .999 fine silver)
- Year and series
- Mintage numbers (lower = more valuable)
- Condition and original packaging
- First year of series often most valuable
- Proof finish vs bullion finish

INVESTMENT NOTES:
- Silver bullion coins valued primarily by metal content
- Proof versions command premium over bullion
- Popular for precious metal investment
- Commemorative proofs can appreciate significantly
- Perth Mint products internationally recognized

MOST VALUABLE SILVER COINS:
• 1990 - First Kookaburra (1kg especially)
• 1996 - First Lunar (Mouse/Rat)
• 2001 - Federation Silver Set
• 2008 - Beijing Olympics Silver Set
• 2015 - ANZAC Centenary
• 2022 - Honey Bee Silver Proof (C mintmark)

NOTES:
- Perth Mint and RAM both produce silver coins
- Kookaburra and Lunar series are world-renowned
- Annual design changes make each year unique
- Certificate of authenticity essential for value
- Many sold in presentation cases
- Popular wedding and anniversary gifts
