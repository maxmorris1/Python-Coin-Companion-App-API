AUSTRALIAN COIN ERRORS & VARIETIES
================================================================================

This file contains all known minting errors and varieties for Australian 
decimal coinage (1966-2026). Error coins are highly collectible and can be 
worth significantly more than face value.

================================================================================
MAJOR DENOMINATION-SPECIFIC ERRORS
================================================================================

5 CENT ERRORS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. 1972 5c "NO SD" ERROR
   Year: 1972
   Description: Missing "SD" designer initials (Stuart Devlin)
   Value: $50-$200+
   Rarity: Uncommon - only some 1972 5c coins affected
   How to identify: Check for absence of "SD" on reverse near echidna
   Keywords: 1972 5c, no SD, missing initials, Stuart Devlin error

2. 1981 5c DIE CRACK
   Year: 1981
   Description: Prominent die crack across echidna design
   Value: $10-$50
   Rarity: Moderate
   Keywords: 1981 5c, die crack, echidna variety

50 CENT ERRORS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3. 1966 50c ROUND SILVER (NOT AN ERROR - DESIGN CHANGE)
   Year: 1966-1967
   Description: Round 80% silver design, replaced in 1969 with dodecagonal
   Value: $10-$50+ (due to silver content and rarity, not error)
   Notes: Withdrawn after silver price rose, not a minting error
   Keywords: 1966 50c, round 50c, silver 50c, 80% silver

4. 2000 50c INCUSE FLAG ERROR
   Year: 2000
   Description: Flag design struck with inverted relief on some coins
   Value: $30-$150
   Rarity: Rare
   Keywords: 2000 50c, incuse flag, millennium error

5. 2019 50c "FROSTED" ANZAC
   Year: 2019
   Description: Frosted finish variety (may be intentional proof-like finish)
   Value: $30-$100+
   Rarity: Uncommon
   How to identify: Matte/frosted appearance vs regular glossy finish
   Keywords: 2019 50c, frosted ANZAC, ANZAC variety, proof-like

$1 DOLLAR ERRORS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

6. 2000 $1/$10 MULE ERROR
   Year: 2000
   Description: $1 coin struck with 10c reverse die (year 2000)
   Value: $1,000-$3,000+
   Rarity: EXTREMELY RARE - less than 10 known examples
   How to identify: $1 coin with lyrebird design instead of kangaroos
   Keywords: 2000 mule, dollar ten cent mule, $1 $10 mule, lyrebird dollar

7. 2007 $1 DOUBLE OBVERSE
   Year: 2007
   Description: Double headed - two obverse sides
   Value: $2,000-$5,000+
   Rarity: EXTREMELY RARE
   Keywords: 2007 double header, $1 double obverse, two heads

$2 DOLLAR ERRORS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

8. 2012 $2 RED POPPY MINTMARK VARIETIES
   Year: 2012
   Description: Various mintmark combinations and missing mintmarks
   Value: $100-$500+ depending on variety
   Rarity: Rare
   Keywords: 2012 red poppy, poppy mintmark, $2 red poppy variety

9. 1988 $2 UNKNOWN VARIETY
   Year: 1988
   Description: First year variety with subtle design differences
   Value: $10-$30
   Rarity: Moderately rare
   Keywords: 1988 $2 variety, aboriginal elder variety

================================================================================
GENERAL MINTING ERRORS (ALL DENOMINATIONS)
================================================================================

DOUBLE STRIKE ERRORS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Coin struck twice, creating doubled image
Affected: All denominations, any year
Value: $100-$2,000+ depending on denomination and clarity
Rarity: Very rare
How to identify: Doubled design elements, often offset
Keywords: double strike, double struck, struck twice

DOUBLE HEADER / DOUBLE TAIL ERRORS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Both sides show same face (two heads or two tails)
Affected: All denominations, any year
Value: $500-$5,000+ depending on denomination
Rarity: Extremely rare
How to identify: Identical sides or both obverse/both reverse
Keywords: double header, double tail, two heads, two tails, wrong die

OFF-CENTER STRIKES:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Coin struck off-center, showing blank planchet area
Affected: All denominations, any year
Value: $50-$500+ depending on percentage off-center
Rarity: Rare (10% off-center), Very rare (50%+ off-center)
How to identify: Design shifted with blank curved edge visible
Keywords: off-center, misaligned strike, shifted strike

CLIPPED PLANCHET:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Coin missing curved section from planchet cutting error
Affected: All denominations, any year
Value: $30-$300+ depending on size of clip
Rarity: Uncommon to rare
How to identify: Crescent-shaped missing section, straight or curved clip
Keywords: clipped planchet, clipped coin, blank clip, straight clip

STRUCK-THROUGH ERRORS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Foreign object between die and coin during striking
Affected: All denominations, any year
Value: $20-$500+ depending on clarity and object
Rarity: Uncommon
How to identify: Ghost image, fabric texture, or smooth blank area
Types: Grease-filled die, cloth, debris, capped die
Keywords: struck through, filled die, grease strike, capped die

DIE CRACKS & CUDS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Raised lines from cracked dies, or "cud" blob from die break
Affected: All denominations, any year
Value: $10-$200+ depending on prominence
Rarity: Common (minor cracks), Uncommon (major cracks), Rare (cuds)
How to identify: Raised line(s) or blob of metal on coin surface
Keywords: die crack, cud, die break, retained cud

BROADSTRIKE:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Coin struck without collar, resulting in oversized diameter
Affected: All denominations, any year
Value: $100-$800+ depending on denomination
Rarity: Rare
How to identify: Larger than normal diameter, thin appearance
Keywords: broadstrike, no collar, expanded coin

WRONG PLANCHET ERRORS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Coin struck on planchet intended for different denomination
Affected: All denominations, any year
Value: $500-$10,000+ depending on combination
Rarity: Extremely rare
Examples:
  - 10c on 5c planchet
  - 20c on 10c planchet
  - $1 on 10c planchet (the famous 2000 mule)
Keywords: wrong planchet, wrong stock, planchet error

MISSING CLAD LAYER:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: One or both cladding layers missing, exposing copper core
Affected: Clad coins (5c, 10c, 20c, 50c)
Value: $100-$1,000+
Rarity: Very rare
How to identify: Copper color showing through, wrong color, thin coin
Types: Missing obverse clad, missing reverse clad, missing both layers
Keywords: missing clad, delamination, clad error, peeling

LAMINATION ERRORS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Metal layers separating or flaking off
Affected: All denominations, any year
Value: $20-$300+ depending on severity
Rarity: Uncommon
How to identify: Peeling metal, flaky surface, layered appearance
Keywords: lamination, peeling, flaking, delamination

BROCKAGE:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Previous coin's impression stamped onto next coin (mirror image)
Affected: All denominations, any year
Value: $200-$2,000+
Rarity: Very rare
How to identify: Mirror/negative image on one side
Keywords: brockage, mirror strike, negative image

ROTATED DIE:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Reverse die rotated relative to obverse (medal vs coin alignment)
Affected: All denominations, any year
Value: $50-$500+ depending on rotation degree
Rarity: Uncommon (slight rotation), Rare (90°+ rotation)
How to identify: Reverse not aligned with obverse when flipped vertically
Note: Australian coins should have "coin alignment" (180° rotation)
Keywords: rotated die, misaligned die, medal alignment

WEAK STRIKE / INSUFFICIENT PRESSURE:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Coin not fully struck, missing detail in high points
Affected: All denominations, any year
Value: $10-$100 (lower value unless extreme)
Rarity: Common (slight), Uncommon (severe)
How to identify: Flat or missing design elements, weak detail
Keywords: weak strike, greased die, worn die, insufficient strike

================================================================================
HOW TO AUTHENTICATE ERROR COINS
================================================================================

GENUINE ERRORS WILL HAVE:
✓ Mint luster (if uncirculated)
✓ Consistent wear pattern (if circulated)
✓ Proper weight and composition
✓ Reeded edge consistent with denomination
✓ Authentic design elements

RED FLAGS FOR FAKE/ALTERED COINS:
✗ Scratched or damaged reeding
✗ Signs of tooling or drilling
✗ Added metal or solder
✗ Artificial coloring or plating
✗ Filed edges
✗ Wrong weight or magnetic properties (Australian coins non-magnetic except $1/$2)

GET EXPERT VERIFICATION:
For high-value errors ($500+), always get authentication from:
- PCGS (Professional Coin Grading Service)
- NGC (Numismatic Guaranty Corporation)
- ANDA (Australasian Numismatic Dealers Association members)

================================================================================
VALUE FACTORS FOR ERROR COINS
================================================================================

MOST VALUABLE:
1. Wrong planchet/Mule errors
2. Double denomination errors
3. Major off-center (50%+ with full date visible)
4. Broadstrikes on higher denominations
5. Brockage errors

MODERATELY VALUABLE:
1. Minor off-center (10-40%)
2. Struck-through with clear detail
3. Major die cracks/cuds
4. Clipped planchets (large clips)
5. Rotated dies (significant rotation)

LOWER VALUE (but still collectible):
1. Minor die cracks
2. Small clips
3. Weak strikes
4. Lamination errors
5. Minor varieties

CONDITION MATTERS:
- Uncirculated errors: 2x-5x value of circulated
- Original luster preserved: Premium value
- Cleaned coins: Significantly reduced value
- Damaged errors: Minimal premium over face value

================================================================================

When searching online (eBay, Gumtree, etc.), use these terms:
• "[Year] [Denomination] error"
• "Australian coin minting error"
• "Off-center Australian coin"
• "Double strike [denomination]"
• "[Year] [Denomination] variety"
• "Mule error Australian"
• "Struck through error"

Always verify before purchasing.

NOTES:
- Not all variations are errors (commemoratives, mintmarks are intentional)
- Environmental damage (PMD - Post Mint Damage) has no premium value
- Dryer coins, magician's coins, and novelty items are not mint errors
- When in doubt, consult an expert before making expensive purchases
- Keep error coins in protective holders to preserve condition
- Document your finds with clear photos and descriptions
