COIN GRADING GUIDE - COMPANIES & SCALES
================================================================================

MAJOR GRADING COMPANIES:
================================================================================

INTERNATIONAL (Most Trusted):
• PCGS (Professional Coin Grading Service) - USA, most popular globally
• NGC (Numismatic Guaranty Corporation) - USA, equally respected as PCGS
• ANACS (American Numismatic Association Certification Service) - USA
• ICG (Independent Coin Grading) - USA

AUSTRALIAN:
• ANDA (Australasian Numismatic Dealers Association)
• Coinworks - Australian dealer grading
• Downtown Coins - Australian dealer grading

EUROPEAN:
• CGS (Certified Grading Service) - UK
• NGC International - European division

NOTE: PCGS and NGC are the most recognized and command highest premiums.
Australian coins graded by PCGS/NGC typically sell for more than local grading.


SHELDON GRADING SCALE (1-70):
================================================================================

The industry standard uses the Sheldon Scale from 1 to 70, where:
• 1 = Poor, barely identifiable
• 70 = Perfect, flawless coin

CIRCULATED GRADES (1-59):
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

POOR TO FAIR:
• PO-1 (Poor 1) - Barely identifiable, major wear
• FR-2 (Fair 2) - Very heavy wear, outline visible
• AG-3 (About Good 3) - Heavy wear, date readable

GOOD:
• G-4 (Good 4) - Heavy wear, major details worn smooth
• G-6 (Good 6) - Heavy wear, slightly better detail

VERY GOOD:
• VG-8 (Very Good 8) - Full rims, some detail visible
• VG-10 (Very Good 10) - Better detail than VG-8

FINE:
• F-12 (Fine 12) - Moderate to heavy wear, major details clear
• F-15 (Fine 15) - Moderate wear, slightly sharper

VERY FINE:
• VF-20 (Very Fine 20) - Moderate wear, most details visible
• VF-25 (Very Fine 25) - Light to moderate wear
• VF-30 (Very Fine 30) - Light wear, most details sharp
• VF-35 (Very Fine 35) - Light wear, sharper than VF-30

EXTREMELY FINE / EXTRA FINE:
• EF-40 / XF-40 (Extremely Fine 40) - Light wear on high points
• EF-45 / XF-45 (Extremely Fine 45) - Very light wear, nearly full detail
• EF-50 / XF-50 (Extremely Fine 50) - Slight wear on highest points only
• EF-55 / XF-55 (Extremely Fine 55) - Minimal wear

ABOUT UNCIRCULATED:
• AU-50 (About Uncirculated 50) - Slight trace of wear, light rub
• AU-53 (About Uncirculated 53) - Very slight wear
• AU-55 (About Uncirculated 55) - Barely any wear, mostly lustrous
• AU-58 (About Uncirculated 58) - Nearly full luster, tiny wear spots


UNCIRCULATED / MINT STATE GRADES (60-70):
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MINT STATE (MS):
• MS-60 (Mint State 60) - No wear, but many contact marks/bag marks
• MS-61 (Mint State 61) - No wear, numerous contact marks
• MS-62 (Mint State 62) - No wear, noticeable contact marks
• MS-63 (Mint State 63) - No wear, some marks visible to naked eye
• MS-64 (Mint State 64) - No wear, few marks, above average luster
• MS-65 (Mint State 65) - No wear, very few marks, excellent eye appeal
• MS-66 (Mint State 66) - No wear, minor marks only, strong luster
• MS-67 (Mint State 67) - Superb, nearly perfect, minimal marks
• MS-68 (Mint State 68) - Nearly flawless, exceptional quality
• MS-69 (Mint State 69) - Almost perfect, one or two tiny imperfections
• MS-70 (Mint State 70) - PERFECT, no flaws even under 5x magnification

PROOF GRADES (PF / PR):
• PF-60 / PR-60 to PF-70 / PR-70 - Same scale as MS, but for proof coins
• Proof coins are specially struck with mirror-like fields
• Most modern proofs are PF-68 to PF-70


COMMON GRADING ABBREVIATIONS & DESIGNATIONS:
================================================================================

BASIC DESIGNATIONS:
• MS = Mint State (uncirculated)
• PF / PR = Proof
• SP = Specimen Strike
• PL = Proof-Like
• DMPL = Deep Mirror Proof-Like
• CAM = Cameo (frosted devices, mirror fields)
• DCAM = Deep Cameo (strong contrast)

COLOR DESIGNATIONS (for copper/bronze):
• RD = Red (95-100% original red color)
• RB = Red-Brown (5-95% red color)
• BN = Brown (little to no red)

SPECIAL LABELS:
• First Strike (FS) - Coins struck within first 30 days
• Early Release (ER) - Similar to First Strike
• Star (★) - PCGS designation for exceptional eye appeal
• Plus (+) - NGC designation for coins at top of grade
• Green CAC Sticker - Premium quality for the grade
• Gold CAC Sticker - Coin should grade higher


AUSTRALIAN COIN GRADING SPECIFICS:
================================================================================

COMMON AUSTRALIAN CIRCULATION GRADES:
• Most Australian coins in pockets: F-12 to VF-30
• Well-preserved pocket change: EF-40 to AU-55
• Roll finds (never circulated): MS-60 to MS-65
• Fresh from RAM: MS-65 to MS-68

AUSTRALIAN PROOF COINS:
• RAM Proof Sets: Typically PF-68 to PF-70
• Silver Proofs: Usually PF-68 to PF-70
• Modern proofs often achieve PF-69 or PF-70

HIGH-GRADE AUSTRALIAN EXAMPLES:
• 1966 Round 50c: MS-65+ worth significant premium
• 1972 5c No SD: MS-65+ extremely rare
• 2012 Red Poppy $2: MS-66+ commands premium
• 2022 Honey Bee C: MS-67+ very collectible


GRADING FOR EBAY SEARCHES:
================================================================================

SEARCH TERMS TO FIND GRADED COINS:
• "PCGS MS65" or "PCGS MS-65"
• "NGC MS67" or "NGC MS-67"
• "ANACS AU58"
• "ICG MS64"
• "Graded" (generic search)
• "Slabbed" (refers to graded coins in protective cases)

PROOF SEARCH TERMS:
• "PCGS PR70" or "PCGS PF70"
• "NGC PF69 DCAM"
• "Proof 70"
• "First Strike PF70"

AUSTRALIAN GRADING SEARCH:
• "PCGS AU" (About Uncirculated)
• "NGC MS" (Mint State)
• "ANDA Graded"

AVOID THESE NON-GRADED TERMS:
• "Looks MS65" = Opinion, not graded
• "High grade" = Not graded
• "Gem BU" = Brilliant Uncirculated, but not certified
• "Choice AU" = Estimated grade, not certified


GRADING PRICE PREMIUMS (GENERAL GUIDE):
================================================================================

MS-60 to MS-63: Minimal premium (1.5x to 2x raw coin)
MS-64 to MS-65: Moderate premium (3x to 5x raw coin)
MS-66 to MS-67: Good premium (5x to 15x raw coin)
MS-68 to MS-69: High premium (15x to 50x raw coin)
MS-70: Exceptional premium (50x to 100x+ raw coin)

NOTE: Premiums vary greatly by rarity, demand, and specific coin.
Population reports (how many graded at each level) affect value significantly.


WHAT GRADE SHOULD YOU BUY?:
================================================================================

FOR INVESTMENT:
• MS-65 to MS-67 (best balance of quality and value)
• PF-69 to PF-70 for proofs
• Always PCGS or NGC for maximum liquidity

FOR COLLECTION:
• MS-63 to MS-65 (good quality, reasonable price)
• AU-58 for older/scarce coins (looks great, costs less)

FOR BUDGET COLLECTORS:
• MS-60 to MS-62 (still uncirculated, affordable)
• VF-EF grades for older coins (presentable, budget-friendly)

FOR REGISTRY SETS:
• MS-68 to MS-70 only (competitive collecting)
• First Strike / Early Release labels add value


USING GRADING IN YOUR EBAY SEARCH:
================================================================================

EXAMPLES:
1. "2012 Red Poppy $2 PCGS MS67" - Specific grade
2. "1966 Round 50 cent NGC MS65" - Graded vintage coin
3. "2022 Honey Bee C PCGS MS68" - Modern collectible, high grade
4. "1972 5c No SD ANACS AU58" - Rare variety, graded
5. "Proof Set 2015 NGC PF70" - Complete proof set graded

TO SPECIFY IN YOUR INPUT:
• Include company + grade: "PCGS MS67"
• Just company: "PCGS" (any grade from that company)
• Just grade level: "MS67" (any company)
• Proof specific: "PF70 DCAM"

KEYWORDS THE SCRAPER RECOGNIZES:
pcgs, ngc, anacs, icg, graded, ms60-ms70, pf60-pf70, pr60-pr70,
ms, pf, pr, proof, first strike, early release
